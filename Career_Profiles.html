📐 STEM Strand — Science, Technology, Engineering & Mathematics
Engineer (General / Civil / Mechanical / Electrical) STEM
Description:

Engineers are among the most in-demand professionals in the Philippines, driving infrastructure, energy, and manufacturing projects. Civil engineers work on roads, bridges, and buildings; electrical and mechanical engineers support manufacturing hubs in Cavite, Laguna, and Batangas.

Salary (PH estimate 2026):

₱25,000–₱150,000+/month. Entry ≈ ₱25–35k; Mid ≈ ₱45–80k; Senior/managerial ≈ ₱90k+. Multinational firms and DPWH contractors pay higher. Metro Manila/private sector pays a premium.

Education / Certification:

BS Engineering (5-year program); must pass the PRC Licensure Examination for Engineers. Board exam required for professional practice. Continuing Professional Development (CPD) credits needed for renewal.

Typical daily tasks:

Reviewing blueprints and technical drawings; conducting site inspections and safety checks; coordinating with contractors and suppliers; preparing engineering reports and cost estimates; attending project status meetings; using AutoCAD or BIM software.

Doctor / Physician STEM
Description:

Physicians are critical to the Philippine healthcare system, with demand across public hospitals (DOH), private hospitals, and OFW-bound medical professionals. Specializations in surgery, internal medicine, and family medicine are most sought after. The Philippines is also a top supplier of doctors to the US and UK.

Salary (PH estimate 2026):

₱40,000–₱250,000+/month. Government/public hospital residents ≈ ₱40–60k; Private practice/specialists ≈ ₱80–200k+; Abroad-bound Filipino doctors can earn PHP equivalent of ₱200k+/month.

Education / Certification:

BS Pre-Med (4 years) + Doctor of Medicine (4 years) + 1-year internship + PRC Medical Board Exam (Physician Licensure Examination). Specialization requires residency (3–5 years) + fellowship.

Typical daily tasks:

Conducting patient consultations and physical examinations; reviewing lab results and diagnostic imaging; prescribing treatments and medications; documenting medical records (EMR systems); collaborating with nurses and allied health staff; attending grand rounds and case conferences.

Commercial Pilot STEM
Description:

Pre-flight inspection and weather briefing; filing flight plans with ATC (CAAP); cockpit checks and instrument calibration; communicating with air traffic control; monitoring fuel consumption and flight systems; completing post-flight reports and logbooks.

Nurse STEM
Description:

Education / Certification:

BS Nursing (4 years); Nurse Licensure Examination (NLE) by PRC; for US deployment: NCLEX-RN; for UK: NMC registration. IELTS/OET required for most international positions.

Typical daily tasks:

Conducting patient assessments and vital signs monitoring; administering medications and IV therapy; coordinating with doctors on patient care plans; documenting patient records in hospital information systems; performing wound care and post-operative monitoring; educating patients and families.

Accountant / CPA ABM
Description:

CPAs are among the most employable professionals in the Philippines. They work in the Big 4 accounting firms (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG), corporations, BIR, COA, SEC, and private businesses. Corporate finance, audit, and tax advisory roles are particularly in demand in Metro Manila's business districts.

Salary (PH estimate 2026):

₱25,000–₱200,000+/month. Entry CPA/auditor ≈ ₱25–40k; Mid-level accounting manager ≈ ₱55–100k; CFO/VP Finance ≈ ₱150–250k+. Big 4 firms and banks pay premium rates.

BS Accountancy (4–5 years); Certified Public Accountant (CPA) Licensure Examination by PRC. Optional: Certified Management Accountant (CMA), ACCA, or CFA for advanced finance roles.

Typical daily tasks:

Preparing financial statements (balance sheet, income statement, cash flow); reconciling accounts and ledgers; filing BIR tax returns (VAT, income tax, withholding tax); conducting internal or external audits; advising on cost optimization; using SAP, QuickBooks, or local accounting software.

Lawyer / Attorney HUMSS
Description:

Lawyers are essential in the Philippines' legal system, practicing in law firms, corporations, government agencies (DOJ, NLRC, courts), and NGOs. The IBP (Integrated Bar of the Philippines) regulates the profession. Specializations in corporate law, labor law, and criminal law are most in demand. The Bar Exam is one of the most rigorous licensure tests in the country.

Salary (PH estimate 2026):

₱30,000–₱300,000+/month. Government lawyer/PAO ≈ ₱30–60k; Associate lawyer (private firm) ≈ ₱45–90k; Senior partner/corporate counsel ≈ ₱150–350k+. In-house counsel in MNCs/banks: ₱100–200k+.

Education / Certification:

Pre-law degree (AB Political Science, AB Philosophy, etc.) + Juris Doctor (JD) — 4 years; Philippine Bar Examination administered by the Supreme Court; IBP membership required for practice.

Typical daily tasks:

Conducting legal research on jurisprudence and statutes; drafting pleadings, contracts, MOAs, and legal opinions; appearing in court hearings (RTC, CA, Supreme Court); meeting with clients to understand legal concerns; negotiating settlements and ADR proceedings; keeping abreast of new laws and SC circulars.
